Tools Required

  • Soldering iron (350°C)
  • Solder (60/40 rosin core)
  • Wire strippers (22 AWG)
  • Flush cutters
  • Digital multimeter
  • Helping hands or PCB vise
  • Heat shrink tubing + heat source
  • Tweezers
  • Small Phillips screwdriver (for trimpot)
Before you begin:
  • NEVER short LiPo battery terminals — fire risk
  • Disconnect battery during all soldering until told to reconnect
  • Polarity matters on capacitors — backwards = explosion risk
  • Set MT3608 voltage BEFORE connecting CYD — overvoltage destroys it
  • Insulate every joint with heat shrink before next stage

Procedure

  1. Strip 3mm off each battery wire
  2. Tin both wire ends and both pads
  3. Solder battery RED to B+ first
  4. Solder battery BLACK to B− second
  5. Apply heat shrink to each joint

Step 2.2 — Add Smoothing Capacitor

Capacitor polarity matters. Long leg = positive (+). Short leg or striped side = negative (−). Reversed = can explode.
Component LegTo
Capacitor LONG leg (+)MT3608 OUT+ pad
Capacitor SHORT leg (−) (striped)MT3608 OUT− pad

Trim legs to ~10mm and solder cap directly to MT3608 output pads.

Step 2.3 — Adjust Output Voltage to 5V

Set voltage BEFORE connecting anything downstream. Overvoltage will destroy the CYD.
  1. Multimeter to DC V, 6000m range
  2. Red probe → MT3608 OUT+ pad
  3. Black probe → MT3608 OUT− pad
  4. Use small screwdriver on brass trimpot
  5. Turn clockwise slowly — may take 15-25 turns to start rising
Test 2.3 — Target Output Voltage
Reading (mV)Action
Below 4900Turn clockwise more
5000 – 5100PASS — STOP HERE
5100 – 5200Acceptable, slightly high
Above 5200Turn counter-clockwise immediately
"OL" displayedSwitch to 60V range, find reading, turn DOWN

6. Stage 4 — CYD Power Input

Why USB Instead of VIN?

The CYD's internal CH340 chip needs the USB connector's V+ pin powered for the ESP32 to boot normally. Powering through VIN causes a boot loop. The micro USB pigtail is the proven fix.

Micro USB Pigtail Layout

Micro USB Male Pigtail (2-Wire)

9. Troubleshooting

CYD Won't Boot — Just Flashes

Likely CauseTestFix
Voltage drop under loadProbe MT3608 OUT while flashingRe-adjust trimpot to 5.05V
Capacitor failure / wrong polarityVisual check stripe placementRe-solder cap correct way
Battery too lowProbe B+ to B−Charge to above 3.7V
Pigtail not seatedWiggle plug, observeRe-seat firmly

Motor Runs Constantly

Likely CauseTestFix
10kΩ pull-down missing or openProbe Gate to GND idleRe-solder pull-down resistor
MOSFET orientation flippedVerify flat side faces youReplace with correct pinout
Damaged MOSFETSwap with new 2N7000Replace

Motor Never Runs

Likely CauseTestFix
Flyback diode reversed (shorting motor)Visual stripe checkReverse diode
GPIO22 wire not connectedProbe Gate during buzzRepair wire
Motor leads reversedSwap motor red/blackRe-solder
1kΩ resistor missingVisualAdd 1kΩ inline with GPIO22

MT3608 Won't Output 5V

Likely CauseTestFix

No input powerProbe IN+ to IN−Fix TP4056 connections
Defective moduleSwap with spare MT3608Replace
